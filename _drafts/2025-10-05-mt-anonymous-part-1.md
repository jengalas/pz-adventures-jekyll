the path around the lake is open, so I will be able to run all the way around the lake sometime soon.  But today, it wasn’t busy when I got over to the point where the “Mt. Anonymous” road branches off, so I scurried into the woods and up the road to check it out.  There are several areas of nice stacked stone retaining walls along the steep switchbacks near the base of the hill, and then higher up I’m pretty sure I found the overlook.  It is a “bumped out” area lined with stones, and there is even still a bit of a view over the lake!  I will have to go back up there with a camera (or at least my phone) to take some photos for you.  I continued up a little farther and then looped around and headed back down.  The road is very rocky and rugged, and although I was hoping it could be a trail run, it turned out to be a hike because it was pretty difficult to maneuver.  When I came back down to the road, I ran just a bit more down toward the dam and then turned around and came back to the parking lot.
